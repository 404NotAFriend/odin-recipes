Francesinha

The best sandwich dinner in the World

The Francesinha, meaning "Little Frenchie" in Portuguese, is an indulgent, hearty sandwich that has become an iconic dish in the city of Porto, Portugal. This unique sandwich is a twist on the French croque-monsieur, adapted to Portuguese tastes.

Ingredients for the Main Dish:

  • 4 slices of sandwich bread
  • 8 slices of cheese
  • 2 sausages (preferably fresh and bought at Bolhão Market "Salsicharia Leandro")
  • 2 linguiças (preferably fresh and bought at Bolhão Market "Salsicharia Leandro")
  • 4 slices of ham
  • Salt and pepper

Ingredients for the sauce:

  • 1 onion and 3 cloves of garlic
  • 4 dl of beer
  • Ripe tomato or tomato pulp
  • 0.5 dl of brandy
  • 0.5 dl of port wine
  • White mature wine
  • Red green wine (optional)
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Mustard
  • Paprika
  • Parsley
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • Fat trimmings or a beef bouillon cube (optional)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Milk as needed
  • Salt and piri-piri as needed

Steps:

  1. First, let's start the recipe with the sauce: peel the onion and garlic, chop them coarsely, and put them in a pot. Meanwhile, add olive oil, bay leaf, paprika, and parsley. Place on the stove and let it sauté for 10 minutes. Add the ripe tomato or tomato pulp, fat trimmings or beef bouillon, beverages, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, and let it boil. Then, dissolve the cornstarch in a bit of milk to cut the acidity and thicken the sauce. Add this mixture to the pot slowly while stirring continuously. Adjust the salt, season with piri-piri, and stir. Next, strain through a fine-mesh sieve and return to low heat to warm up.

  2. Second, assemble the Francesinha: On a slightly toasted slice of bread, place the ham, steak, a layer of previously grilled linguiça and sausage, and finally another slice of bread.

  3. Finally, cover the sandwich with 4 slices of cheese. Place it in the oven at 200°C (392°F) until the cheese melts, then remove and serve hot, drizzled with the sauce.

Note: Serve with French fries. You can also add other types of sausages to the filling according to your preference, such as cured pork loin, mortadella, bacon, etc. Finish with a fried egg on top of the Francesinha.
